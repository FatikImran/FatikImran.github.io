Research Experience

Parallel Triangle Counting on Shared-Memory Systems

Implemented and evaluated a high-performance triangle-counting system in C++17 for large undirected graphs, combining SIMD and OpenMP techniques on shared-memory hardware. Developed six variants (scalar, SIMD intrinsics, OpenMP SIMD, OpenMP threads, and two hybrid approaches), architecture-aware configuration, and a reproducible benchmarking pipeline on SNAP datasets. Applied degree-based orientation and preprocessing to ensure each triangle is counted exactly once, and achieved up to ~7.3x speedup versus scalar baseline on tested datasets.

C++17 OpenMP SIMD/AVX2 Parallel Computing Graph Analytics Performance Engineering

Methodology inspired by prior work from Zhang et al. (HPEC 2018) and Tom et al. (2017).
